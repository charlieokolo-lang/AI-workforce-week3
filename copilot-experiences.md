**Copilot Chat** is a conversational AI interface that allows users to interact with Copilot through natural language to ask questions, summarise content, draft text, and retrieve information. It is available across Microsoft 365 apps and via the web, and it can reference organisational data when the user is signed in with a work account under a qualifying licence. You would use Copilot Chat for everyday tasks such as summarising a long email thread, generating a first draft of a document, or quickly finding information without leaving your current application.

**Copilot for Business**, more formally known as **Microsoft 365 Copilot**, is the fully integrated enterprise experience where Copilot is embedded directly within apps like Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams, and Outlook. It goes beyond simple chat by actively participating in your workflow, drafting reports in Word, building formulas in Excel, generating presentations in PowerPoint, and summarising meetings in Teams in real time.

You would use Microsoft 365 Copilot when you need AI assistance that is deeply contextual, drawing on your organisation's emails, meetings, files, and calendar to produce relevant and accurate outputs within your day-to-day tools. 

Copilot Studio is a low-code development platform that allows organisations to build, customise, and deploy their own Copilot agents and automated workflows tailored to specific business needs. It enables the creation of agents that can answer domain-specific questions, integrate with external data sources, and automate repetitive processes across departments. 

You would use Copilot Studio when out-of-the-box Copilot experiences are insufficient, and your organisation requires a custom AI solution, for example, an HR onboarding assistant, a project management bot, or an environmental compliance adviser. In essence, Copilot Chat is for everyday interaction, Microsoft 365 Copilot is for deep workplace integration, and Copilot Studio is for building tailored AI-powered solutions.
