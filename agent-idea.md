**Agent Name: DesignBridge - UI/UX Design Assistant for Environmental & Engineering Platforms**

The problem this agent aims to solve is the disconnect that often exists between technical environmental and engineering data and the digital interfaces through which users interact with that data.

Many environmental platforms, civil infrastructure dashboards, and sustainability reporting tools are poorly designed, overwhelming users with raw data and complex navigation that reduces usability, accessibility, and overall user satisfaction.

**DesignBridge** is a Copilot agent designed for UI/UX designers, product teams, civil engineers, and environmental professionals who are involved in building or improving digital tools, platforms, and dashboards related to infrastructure, sustainability, or environmental management. It is particularly valuable for designers working on data-heavy applications who need guidance on translating complex technical content into clean, intuitive, and accessible user experiences.

The agent should be able to answer questions such as "What is the best way to present environmental impact data on a dashboard for non-technical users?" or "How should I structure the user flow for an environmental compliance reporting tool?" It should handle tasks such as generating UI/UX design recommendations, suggesting accessible colour schemes and typography choices, and producing user journey maps or wireframe descriptions based on a given brief.

DesignBridge should assist users in writing UX research plans, drafting usability testing scripts, and identifying pain points in existing interface designs through heuristic evaluation prompts. The agent should also help teams align their design decisions with recognised standards such as WCAG accessibility guidelines and Nielsen's usability heuristics, ensuring that platforms are both inclusive and effective. It should support the creation of personas, user stories, and interaction design rationale documents tailored to environmental or engineering contexts.

Additionally, DesignBridge should help bridge communication between design teams and technical stakeholders by generating plain-language design rationale documents and translating engineering requirements into user-centred design briefs. By building this agent, the goal is to elevate the quality of digital experiences within environmental and engineering sectors, empowering designers and engineers alike to create platforms that are not only functionally robust but also intuitive, accessible, and human-centred.
